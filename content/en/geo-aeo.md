## What GEO promotion is

GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is content optimization for generative engines: Yandex Neuro and Alice, ChatGPT, Perplexity. The goal is that when a client asks a question, the neural network cites your site and names your brand among recommendations, not only competitors. The same intent is searched as “promotion in neural networks,” “AI promotion,” or “AI SEO.” Market terminology is not settled yet, but the substance is one.

## What AEO is and how it differs from GEO

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) is optimization for “answer” search mechanics: direct answers, ready-solution blocks, voice assistants. AEO focuses on structure, that is a short precise answer under every question. GEO is broader: it adds work on brand citability in generative answers and on the sources from which neural networks assemble a market picture. In practice we do them together. AEO structure is the foundation; brand citability is the result.

## How AI chooses whom to cite

Almost nobody writes about this, so we explain the mechanic. We [build language-model systems ourselves](/uslugi/vnedrenie-ii/) and know it from the inside. A generative answer is assembled in three steps:

1. **Candidate selection.** On the user’s query the engine runs a normal search and takes visible pages. For Neuro that is practically the top 20/30 of Yandex results, so a site invisible in search does not become a candidate.
2. **Fragment extraction.** From candidate pages the model pulls chunks that directly answer the question: self-contained paragraphs, table rows, list items. Vague copy “about high-quality services” has nothing to extract; there is no answer in it.
3. **Synthesis and attribution.** From extracted fragments the model assembles the answer and names sources and brands. The more often a brand appears in mutually consistent sources, the higher the chance it enters the answer wording.

All GEO practice follows from this mechanic: be in the visible search zone, write in extractable blocks, and grow consistent brand mentions both on your site and beyond it.

## How content gets into an AI answer

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/geo-aeo-2.svg | Content path into a neural network answer]
[IMG: ../assets/images/geo-aeo-1.jpg | Content that AI cites]

The conditions are known from GEO practitioners’ experience and our own tests:

1. **Visibility in classic search.** The page must be in the top 20/30 for the query, because neural networks take candidates from there.
2. **Answer-first structure.** A direct 40–60 word answer sits right under a question heading so that paragraph can be “cut out” into an answer without losing meaning.
3. **Formats AI likes to cite.** Tables of “price / timeline / what’s included,” numbered lists, FAQ.
4. **Markup.** FAQPage, Article, Organization give machine-readable structure.
5. **Freshness.** Content is updated and carries update dates, at least quarterly.
6. **Access for AI crawlers.** Open robots and llms.txt, a site navigator file for neural networks. This is community practice, and we set it up.
7. **E-E-A-T.** Author, sources for figures, domain trust.

## Which pages AI cites more readily

Neural networks cite not “sites” but specific pages, and not all page types are equally useful. Most often answers pull service pages with direct answers and “price / timeline / what’s included” tables, FAQ sections, expert guides and reviews, glossaries with term definitions, and “how to choose” / “how to do” methods. Least often cited are homepage showcases and “about our professionalism” copy without specifics. The practical takeaway is simple: for valuable niche questions the site needs separate citable pages, not one general text. If an AI pipeline prepares content for them, the link matters. We build [sites with AI content](/uslugi/razrabotka-saitov/sait-s-ii/) that pass both into search and into citations.

## Off-site GEO: mentions beyond the site

A neural network assembles a market picture not only from your site. Candidates include review sites, rankings and “best of” roundups, industry media, Habr and vc.ru, directories and maps. If for “best X companies” AI cites three roundups that omit you, your own site will not win that fight, because you need to be in the roundups themselves. So GEO work includes an audit of external sources. We find which platforms AI cites in your niche, where the brand is missing or described wrong, and build a presence plan: cards and reviews, publications, fixing outdated data. Mentions must be consistent, because mismatch in names, addresses, and positioning lowers the model’s confidence in the brand.

“It got better” does not count as a metric, so measurement is formalized:

1. **Fixed query set.** We take 20–50 real client questions about the niche, agree them at the start, and do not change them between measurements.
2. **Several engines.** Each query goes to Neuro, ChatGPT, and Perplexity, because engines have different sources and different answers.
3. **Mention capture.** For each answer we note whether the brand is named, whether the site is cited, and which competitors appear.
4. **Monthly dynamics.** We count brand and competitor mention share by month. You see what grows, what slips, and where to move content.
5. **Business reconciliation.** We look at transitions from AI sources in Metrica and branded demand dynamics, because citability must convert.

## FAQ

**What are GEO and AEO in plain words?**
GEO is work so that neural networks (Neuro, ChatGPT, Perplexity) cite your brand in answers. AEO gives the structural base for that: direct answers under question headings. Together they turn the site into a source AI chooses to cite.

**How does GEO differ from regular SEO?**
SEO fights for a position in a list of links; GEO wins a mention in the answer text itself. SEO success is measured by positions and traffic; GEO success by brand mention share in niche answers. They work together, because GEO is built on SEO visibility.

**Can you get into AI answers without search positions?**
By market practice almost never, because Neuro takes candidates from the top 20/30 of ordinary results. If the site is invisible in search, start with an SEO foundation. Treat a promise to “get into AI without positions” as a contractor red flag.

**How do we check whether AI cites us?**
Ask Neuro and ChatGPT 5–10 real client questions about your service and see whose brands appear. We do this by protocol: niche query set, several engines, mention capture. The first check is free.

**Which site pages does AI cite most often?**
Pages with extractable answers: services with “price / timeline” tables, FAQ sections, guides, glossaries, “how to choose” methods. Showcase copy without specifics is cited worst. Valuable niche questions need separate answer pages.

**What is off-site GEO and why work external platforms?**
Neural networks assemble the answer not only from your site. They use review sites, “best of” roundups, media, Habr. If AI cites rankings that omit you, you need to get into the rankings. Audit and an external-mention plan are part of the service.

**Does this work for both Yandex Neuro and ChatGPT?**
Yes, base principles are shared: answer-first structure, markup, accessibility, authority. Differences are in the details. For Neuro, Yandex visibility is critical; for ChatGPT and Perplexity, AI crawler access and source citability matter more.

**What is llms.txt?**
A site navigator file for neural networks: a short company description and links to key pages in an AI-friendly format. For now it is community practice, not an official standard, but it is cheap and useful. We set it up in the base package.

**Do we need to rewrite the whole site?**
No, we start with 5–10 pages for the most valuable niche questions. Usually restructuring existing texts is enough: question headings, direct answers, and tables, not writing from scratch.

**How much does GEO promotion cost?**
Two formats: a module on SEO, or a standalone project for sites with strong organic. We price individually by niche. The first citability check is free. For the decision, calculate what invisibility in a channel with a 13M+ daily audience costs you.

**How fast will results appear?**
With ready visibility, faster than classic SEO: restructured content enters answers after reindexing, measured in weeks. Without visibility, timelines are set by the SEO foundation, usually 3–6 months for long-tail queries.

**How is the result measured?**
By a monthly protocol: fixed set of 20–50 niche queries → Neuro/ChatGPT/Perplexity answers → your brand and competitor mention share. Plus business reconciliation: transitions from AI sources in Metrica, branded demand.
