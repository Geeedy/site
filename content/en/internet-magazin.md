## There are marketplaces: why your own store

Not “or,” but “and.” Marketplaces bring traffic but take commission, own the client base, and change rules without you. Your own store gives margin without commission, a buyer base for repeat sales, and independence from someone else’s rules. The working setup: marketplaces for reach, your store for profit and loyal customers.

## FAQ

**How much does an online store cost?**
By class: niche test roughly 200–300k RUB, SMB store roughly 300–700k RUB, systemic with integrations from roughly 1.5M RUB, highload roughly 4–6M+ RUB. Market median roughly 650k–2M RUB. We calculate the estimate individually. Class and calculation return in 2 days.

**How long does development take?**
A basic store takes 2–4 weeks, studio SMB 2–3 months, large with 1C and warehouses 4–6 months or more. Timeline locks after the prototype. Staged launch shortens time to first sales.

**Which platform do you use?**
The platform is chosen by project class and growth plans. Principles are constant: product-card speed, convenient admin, no lock-in to us. Code and access are handed over fully.

**Is 1C and warehouse integration included?**
For the systemic class yes, that is its essence: stock, prices, reserves, and documents sync automatically. For the SMB class, basic catalog sync is available. Integration depth locks on the prototype.

**Which payment and delivery methods do you connect?**
Cards, Faster Payments System (SBP), invoices for legal entities, installments per your provider contracts. For delivery: courier services, pickup points, self-pickup, tracking, and client notifications. The set depends on your logistics.

**There are marketplaces: why your own store?**
For margin without marketplace commission, your own client base, repeat sales, and independence from someone else’s rules. Working setup: marketplaces give reach; your store brings profit and loyal customers.

**What do AI search and recommendations give?**
Search understands free wording and typos, and the client finds the product instead of leaving. Recommendations lift average order value. Per industry data, AI personalization adds 15–30% to conversion.

**Who fills the catalog?**
Initial load we do, from your data or 1C. Then the catalog lives by sync with the accounting system, and cards update without manual work. Admin lets your team manage without a programmer.

**What about SEO for a store?**
Category structure for demand, product markup, speed, and clean indexing are part of development. Promotion starts on launch day. For stores SEO pays back fast thanks to hot transactional demand.

**Will the store handle a sale?**
Project class is chosen for peak load, and for systemic and highload stores load testing is part of launch. If you start with a small class, architecture is designed so growth does not require a rebuild from scratch.
