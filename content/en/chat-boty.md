## How an AI bot differs from a button bot

An AI bot understands free-text questions and answers from the company knowledge base: price lists, policies, service descriptions. A button bot walks a rigid “press 1” tree; one step off the path and the client is stuck. An AI bot answers the question in any wording and hands atypical cases to an operator with dialogue history.

## How we launch a bot

1. **Knowledge base.** We gather price lists, policies, and frequent questions so the bot answers from them, not invents.
2. **Escalation scenarios.** We define what the bot resolves itself and what it hands to a human.
3. **Pilot on part of traffic.** We review real dialogues.
4. **Training on dialogues.** The base grows with live questions, and accuracy rises weekly.

**From practice.** We start with the knowledge base, not scenarios, because 80% of bot quality is the completeness of service and policy descriptions. Scenarios without a base make a pretty presentation and a useless bot.

## FAQ

**How does an AI bot differ from a button bot?**
An AI bot understands free wording and answers from your knowledge base; a button bot walks a rigid menu. An AI bot does not trap the client: an atypical question it closes from the base or hands to an operator with full dialogue context.

**How many inquiries can a bot close?**
Market-wide 66% of typical requests (Markswebb); in mature rollouts up to 75%. Your figure depends on knowledge-base completeness. Realistically start with half of typical topics and grow as the bot learns from live dialogues.

**Builder or custom development: what to choose?**
A builder (from roughly 630 RUB/month) covers typical scenarios DIY. Custom development is needed when the bot must know your policies, write to CRM, and handle non-standard logic. We will compare both options on the demo; sometimes the honest answer is “a builder is enough.”

**How much does an AI chatbot cost?**
Builders cost roughly 630–2,000 RUB/month. Custom development for SMB costs, by market estimates, roughly 80–500k RUB and depends on channels, knowledge base, and integrations. We price individually. The estimate is locked after scoping the tasks.

**Which channels does the bot work in?**
Website widget, Telegram, WhatsApp, MAX, and voice. One knowledge base across channels, so answers stay consistent and a new channel connects without rebuilding the bot.

**How do leads from the bot get into CRM?**
Automatically: the bot creates a deal with source, topic, and dialogue history, qualifies by your questions, and assigns an owner. The manager continues from CRM; the client does not repeat everything.

**How fast can we launch?**
Most time goes to the knowledge base. With ready price lists and policies, launch usually takes from two weeks. A pilot on part of traffic lets you start earlier and keep training the bot on live dialogues.

**What does the bot answer at night and on weekends?**
The same as daytime: full answers from the knowledge base, booking, request intake. Questions that need a human are logged with a promise to reply in business hours. The request is already in CRM, not lost.

**Will the bot handle Russian language and slang?**
Yes, modern language models understand conversational wording, typos, and abbreviations. Accuracy on your topic comes from the knowledge base and ongoing training on real client dialogues.

**Where is dialogue data stored?**
In your perimeter or on Russian platforms; architecture is chosen with 152-FZ in mind. Personal data is not sent to external services without your decision. Access and logs stay under your control.

**Chatbot and voice robot: the same thing?**
One technology, different channels: text and voice. We build voice as an extension of the text bot: first the bot learns from chat, then the same brain answers by phone. More on the [voice bots](/uslugi/golosovye-boty/) page.

**How does the bot learn from dialogues?**
Each week we review dialogues where the bot failed and expand the knowledge base. Accuracy grows week to week, and this is included in support, not sold separately.
