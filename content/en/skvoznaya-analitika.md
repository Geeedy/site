Metrica shows clicks and visits; money comes from deals. Between click and payment a lead passes through the website, CRM, and sales, and without end-to-end analytics that path stays a dark corridor: budget is allocated by feel. ## What end-to-end analytics is in plain language

End-to-end analytics is a reporting system that links ad spend to revenue from deals. It unites data from ad accounts, the website, CRM, and cash into one chain and shows the customer path from first click to payment, including repeat sales.

## How end-to-end analytics works

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/skvoznaya-analitika-1.svg | Data path: from click to money]

The mechanic has four links. Ads pass UTM tags and spend. The website records the visit and turns a visitor into a lead via a form, chat, or call with call tracking. CRM moves the lead through the funnel to deal and payment. The analytics system glues it all into a report where each channel shows cost per lead, cost per deal, and return on investment. A break in any link ruins the picture, so implementation is not “install a counter” but build a complete data chain.

## What it shows: metrics

Main indicators: CPL (cost per lead), CPO (cost per order/deal), ROMI (return on marketing investment), and LTV (lifetime value of a customer). A typical first-month insight looks like this: the channel with the cheapest leads brings applications that never reach payment, while an “expensive” channel brings deals with better economics. Without end-to-end analytics that channel would have been turned off for being expensive.

## Four maturity stages of analytics

Honest takeaway from the ladder: if ad spend is under roughly 100,000 RUB per month and there is one channel, start with stages 1–2 for free. Services and implementation pay back across several channels and meaningful budgets.

## Attribution models: which to choose

Attribution is the rule for which channel gets credit for a deal when the customer touched several. Last click is simple and fits short sales cycles; first click values awareness channels; a linear model splits value across touchpoints. For a start, last click with a manual check of long deals is almost always enough; complicate the model only when the team trusts the base figures.

## FAQ

**What is end-to-end analytics in plain language?**
A reporting system that links ad spend to deal revenue: it unites ad accounts, website, CRM, and cash and shows the path from click to payment. It answers “which channel pays back.”

**How does it differ from Yandex Metrica?**
Metrica sees visits and site conversions; end-to-end analytics adds money: the lead path in CRM to deal and payment. Metrica answers “where traffic comes from”; end-to-end analytics answers “which channel makes profit.”

**Which metrics does it show?**
Cost per lead (CPL) and per deal (CPO) by channel, return on investment (ROMI), lifetime customer value (LTV). Plus lead quality: which sources bring applications that reach payment.

**Which service to choose: Roistat, Calltouch, or CoMagic?**
It depends on channels, traffic volume, and CRM. Roistat is strong on integrations (from 11,700 RUB/month), Calltouch on call tracking (from 990 RUB/month), CoMagic on telephony (from 4,900 RUB/month). We pick for the task at the audit, without partner bias.

**Can we set it up for free?**
Yes, base level: UTM tagging plus a CRM source report, or Yandex Metrica linked with spend upload and CRM data. For one or two channels that is enough. Services pay back across several channels.

**What does implementation cost?**
By market: setup with CRM integration 50,000–150,000 RUB; complex implementation 80,000–250,000 RUB; service subscription from roughly 1,000 to 20,000+ RUB/month. We price individually after a free audit; the estimate is fixed before start.

**Why is CRM needed for end-to-end analytics?**
Money data lives in CRM. If deals are created intermittently and sources are lost, reports lie with any service. So implementation includes CRM order: rules, required fields, discipline oversight.

**What is an attribution model, and which to choose?**
The rule for which channel gets credit for a deal with multiple touchpoints. For a start, last click (the last click before the lead) is enough; complicate only after the team trusts the base figures.

**Do we need call tracking?**
If clients call, yes: without number substitution calls will not bind to channels, and a large share of leads will fall out of reports. If there are no calls, call tracking is not needed and you can take a cheaper service.

**When is end-to-end analytics not needed?**
When there is one channel and a small budget: UTM tagging and a CRM report are enough. Implementation pays back across several channels and budgets where 15–30% savings covers system cost.

**What are implementation timelines?**
Base setup takes 2–4 weeks; complex implementation with CRM order 1–2 months. The first reliable report appears after a full deal cycle in your niche.

**How do we know analytics is showing a lie?**
Signs: the report disagrees with cash, half of deals have source “direct,” managers fill CRM after the fact. That is fixed with funnel order, and our audit starts with exactly those checks.
