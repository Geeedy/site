## What a business process audit is

A business process audit is a systematic breakdown of how the company does work: who does what, in what sequence, and at what cost. The audit finds bottlenecks, duplicates, and waste, and in our version also answers the main question: which processes are worth handing to AI and how much that saves.

## When a company needs an audit

Across aggregated surveys of Russian companies, up to 30–50% of employee time goes to routine: manual data entry, hunting for information, duplicating operations across systems, and email approvals. You need an audit if you recognize yourself in at least one of five situations:

1. **You plan automation or AI** and do not know which process to start with.
2. **The company is growing**, but processes stayed from when headcount was half as large.
3. **You already tried automation**, but the pilot gave no effect and was shut down.
4. **Processes live in people’s heads**, and one employee leaving stops a department.
5. **Costs grow faster than revenue**, and it is unclear where team hours go.

## Method: four steps from map to strategy

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/audit-ii-strategiya-1.svg | Audit method: four steps]

1. **Process map (AS IS).** Team interviews and system review. We document how work runs now, in standard BPMN notation.
2. **Economics of each process.** We calculate by formula: employee hours × rate + cost of errors. Each process gets a monthly cost.
3. **Prioritization by AI payback.** We match processes to measured AI effects: support payroll costs down 30–40%, document processing 70–80% cheaper, decisions 20–40% faster. We rank candidates by payback period.
4. **AI strategy (TO BE).** A document with the target state: what we automate, in what order, with which expected figures and risks.

## Internal or external auditor

An internal audit is cheaper and knows context, but it has two weak spots: employees struggle to see familiar chaos from outside, and a department head struggles to write that their processes are inefficient. An external auditor looks without politics and compares you with practice at other companies. The working scheme is external method and calculation plus your internal process knowledge.

## FAQ

**What is a business process audit?**
A systematic breakdown of how the company does work: who does what, in what sequence, and at what cost. Our audit adds calculation: what each process costs and where AI pays back. Output: process map, economics, and strategy.

**How does your audit differ from classic consulting?**
By the result. Consulting describes processes and recommends “optimize”; we calculate economics per process and rank automation candidates by payback period. And we implement ourselves, so we own the feasibility of our recommendations.

**What is an AI strategy and why do we need it?**
A document with priorities: which processes to automate, in what order, with what expected economics. Per surveys, only 26% of companies have one. Without a strategy rollouts go pointwise, and 30–40% of pilots shut down without effect.

**What method does the audit use?**
Market standard: document current state (AS IS) in BPMN, then design the target (TO BE). Our addition is an economic layer: price of each process by “hours × rate + errors” and prioritization by AI payback.

**What does it cost and what are the timelines?**
Market ranges: 80,000–250,000 RUB and 1–3 weeks for companies up to 100 employees; 250,000–600,000 RUB and 1–2 months for mid-market. We price individually by scope. An audit of one process is free. The estimate is fixed before start.

**What do I get in hand?**
A process map with bottlenecks, an economics table (price of each process now and with AI), and an AI strategy with priorities for 6–12 months. Documents are self-contained; you can execute with any contractor.

**What does a free audit of one process include?**
You describe the process in your own words; we break it down on a call, calculate economics, and return a conclusion: whether AI pays back there and what to do next. That is enough to judge the method before any payment.

**How is savings calculated?**
By formula: employee hours on the process × rate + cost of errors, minus implementation and support cost. All variables come from your data at the audit; after the pilot the calculation is checked against fact.

**What do you need from us for the audit?**
Access to people and numbers: 1–2 hours of interviews with process owners, operation stats (how many leads, documents, calls per month), role rates. Policies help but are not required, because often they simply do not exist.

**What happens after the audit?**
Three honest paths. You execute the strategy yourself or with another contractor; the report is concrete enough for that. You run a pilot with us on the strategy’s first priority. Or you pause, and the strategy waits for budget without losing relevance.

**Does the audit obligate us to buy implementation from you?**
No. The report is yours; the plan can be executed with anyone. We earn from those who choose to implement with us after the audit, and there are enough of them that we do not turn the audit into manipulation.

**Does it fit small business?**
Yes, as a free audit of one process or a short audit of 2–3 key processes. Small business often needs AI more: every saved hour is more visible, and process chaos is more expensive.
