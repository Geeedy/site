## What AI implementation gives a business

AI implementation means handing typical company operations (handling inquiries, documents, requests, reports) to programs built on language models. Employees stay on complex cases and oversight. The effect is measured in money: lower payroll cost on routine work, faster processing, fewer errors.

This is already statistics, not a promise: [94% of companies that adopted AI report lower operating costs, and 83% report revenue growth](https://upr.ru/article/generativnyy-ii-v-kompaniyakh-chast-2-generativnyy-ii-v-rossii-2025-2026/).

## Why 30–40% of AI pilots fail

According to market surveys, [30–40% of AI pilot projects in Russia are shut down](https://ict.moscow/analytics/v-krupnykh-kompaniiakh-do-stadii-vnedreniia-doshli-do-10-pilotnykh-proektov-po-ii/) without a financial effect. The reasons are known: the pilot is launched “for AI’s sake” without a business metric, the solution is not embedded in real processes, data turns out raw, and the project has no business-side owner.

We design the process so these four reasons do not apply:

1. **Metric before kickoff.** The pilot starts from locked “before” numbers, not a slide deck.
2. **Process, not a demo.** AI is embedded in real work from day one of the pilot, in the same systems people already use.
3. **Honest data audit.** If data is raw, we start with a process where data is already accumulated.
4. **Owner on your side.** We appoint one together at kickoff; without them, we do not take the project.

## How implementation works: 5 steps

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/vnedrenie-ii-2.svg | How implementation works: from audit to scale]

1. **Audit (1–2 weeks).** We break down processes and calculate the economics of each.
2. **Choose the process with maximum ROI.** We lock “before” metrics and success criteria.
3. **Pilot (usually 2–6 weeks; exact timing depends on the process).** AI runs on a limited volume under your employees’ control.
4. **Measure “after” metrics.** We compare hours, cost per operation, and error counts with “before.”
5. **Scale.** We expand to adjacent processes. Payment by stage.

[IMG: ../assets/images/vnedrenie-ii-2.jpg | Business process audit before an AI pilot]

**From practice.** The most common mistake is starting with the hardest process. We start where the effect is visible in weeks. That builds team trust and funds the next stages.

## FAQ

**What does AI implementation give a business?**
Lower cost of routine work: AI closes 60–80% of typical inquiries, processes documents 70–80% cheaper, and takes load off staff. Market statistics: 94% of adopters report cost reduction, 83% report revenue growth.

**How much does AI implementation cost?**
Market ranges: simple pilot roughly 300k–1.5M RUB, mid-size with integrations roughly 1.5–5M RUB. We price each process individually, with staged payments. As a rule of thumb, a pilot costs less than a month of the process being automated. The estimate is locked after the audit.

**Which process should we start with?**
One with many repeatable operations, a clear cost per hour, and accumulated data. Usually support, request handling, or document workflow. At the audit we calculate economics for all candidates and recommend the one that pays back fastest.

**How long does a pilot take?**
Audit takes 1–2 weeks; a pilot usually runs 2–6 weeks depending on the process and data readiness. You leave with working AI on a real process and a before/after metric comparison.

**Why do AI pilots fail, and what do you do differently?**
Market-wide, 30–40% of pilots are shut down: no business metric, solution not embedded in processes, raw data, no owner. Our safeguards: metric locked before kickoff, AI runs in real systems from day one, data checked at audit, owner appointed at kickoff.

**What data is needed to start?**
Policies, inquiry or document history, access to process systems: CRM, 1C, email, telephony. If data is scarce, we choose a process where it is already accumulated and grow the knowledge base during the pilot.

**Is this legal under 152-FZ?**
Yes, with the right architecture: Russian models or open models in your perimeter; personal data does not go to external services. 152-FZ requirements are built into the project at audit, not after launch.

**Will AI replace my employees?**
AI takes routine work: standard replies, data entry, first-pass processing. People move to complex cases, oversight, and client work. In practice, companies more often redistribute the team and grow without proportional hiring than they cut headcount.

**How do you calculate payback?**
Simple formula: employee hours on the process × rate + cost of errors, minus implementation and support cost. All variables are locked at audit; after the pilot, plan is checked against fact. You see the calculation before you pay for implementation.

**What if the pilot shows no effect?**
That risk is closed at audit: we do not take a process into pilot if the economics do not add up. If fact diverges from the estimate, we diagnose causes and either adjust the solution or stop honestly; you do not pay to scale something unproven.

**How are you different from boxed AI services?**
A box solves one typical scenario and does not know your processes. We implement AI in your systems, on your data, with metrics for your process. Where a box beats custom build on economics, we recommend the box honestly, because economics decide.

**What if we have no IT department?**
Nothing extra on your side: we cover the engineering end-to-end; you need a process owner from the business. Surveys show AI talent shortage holds back 49% of companies; an external team solves that without hiring.
