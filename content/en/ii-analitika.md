# AI Analytics: Ask Your Data in Plain Language

Company numbers sit in CRM, 1C, Yandex Metrica, and a dozen spreadsheets, while a simple question gets answered “by Friday.” An AI analyst answers in minutes: you ask in plain language, the agent finds data in your systems and returns an answer with figures and a chart. It is an analyst who does not resign, get sick, or tire of the same questions.

## 10 questions you will ask the agent on day one

1. How many leads did the website bring this month, and what is conversion to deal?
2. What is revenue by each line versus last quarter?
3. Which managers closed the most deals, and whose funnel is stuck?
4. Which products leave the warehouse faster than we restock them?
5. How much did a lead cost from each ad channel?
6. Which clients have not bought in more than six months?
7. Where are we losing margin, by which categories and why?
8. What is the sales forecast for next month?
9. Which invoices have been unpaid for more than 30 days?
10. What changed this week that I should know about?

If you currently answer at least three of these with exports and manual pivots, the agent will pay back quickly.

## What an AI analyst can do

An AI analyst is an agent connected to company data. It understands a plain-language question, gathers data from systems, and returns an answer with figures, a table, or a chart.

[IMG: ../assets/images/ii-analitika-1.jpg | Team discussing data gathered by an AI analyst]

### Answers questions
It takes any question about the data, without SQL, without exports, without waiting for an analyst.

### Builds scheduled reports
The Monday sales summary arrives on its own, assembled from CRM, 1C, and Metrica.

### Forecasts
It builds sales and demand forecasts. ML forecasts are [10–30% more accurate than traditional methods](https://korusconsulting.ru/blog/tsifrovaya-transformatsiya/ii-dlya-biznesa-kak-i-zachem-vnedryat-iskusstvennyy-intellekt-v-2025-2026-godu/).

### Spots anomalies
A sharp rise in refusals, a conversion drop, atypical spend. The agent reports on its own, without waiting for a question.

### Unifies sources
One answer from several systems: ads from Metrica, deals from CRM, payments from 1C. For inbound-lead assessment there is a related tool, [lead scoring](/uslugi/ii-agenty/dlya-prodazh/).

## How it differs from BI dashboards

A dashboard answers the questions guessed at setup time. One step aside and you need a rebuild. The agent answers any question about connected data: today about conversion by manager, tomorrow about margin by category. Dashboards are not cancelled; the agent complements them, covering the “tail” of non-standard questions that dashboards are not rebuilt for.

## FAQ

**Which data can we connect?**
CRM (Bitrix24, amoCRM), 1C, Yandex Metrica, ad accounts, Excel and Google Sheets. Connection via API in read-only mode. Connector list for your stack we confirm on the demo.

**How accurate are forecasts?**
ML demand and sales forecasts are 10–30% more accurate than traditional methods per industry data. Accuracy on your data depends on history and quality. We show it on a pilot, not promise it in advance.

**How does an AI analyst differ from BI dashboards?**
A dashboard answers anticipated questions; the agent answers any question about connected data. Dashboards stay for monitoring key metrics; the agent covers the tail of non-standard questions without rebuilds.

**Who sees our data?**
Only you. The agent runs in your perimeter or on Russian hosting; access is limited to assigned sources in read-only mode; queries and answers are logged. Personal data is processed under 152-FZ.

**What does it cost?**
It depends on sources and data complexity; we price individually. For comparison, take the full cost of an in-house analyst. Demo on your data is free; you see value before a contract.

**How fast is it implemented?**
First answers appear within a few days after sources are connected; exact timing depends on access. Most time goes to access and data-quality checks. Regular reports and anomalies are set up in the second week.

**What questions can we ask?**
Any that connected data can answer: sales, leads, inventory, finance, marketing. The agent honestly says when there is no data for an answer, and that is better than a pretty invented figure.

**What if data is dirty?**
The agent shows conflicts already on the demo: CRM vs 1C mismatches, duplicates, gaps. Often that is the first project value. Then either we clean data together, or the agent works with reliability caveats.

**Do we still need an in-house analyst after this?**
If you have none, the agent covers most tasks without hiring. If you have one, the agent frees them from routine exports for deeper research. The agent strengthens the function; it does not ban the profession.

**Does it work with Excel spreadsheets?**
Yes, spreadsheets connect as a source alongside systems. In practice the agent quickly shows why sheets disagree, and that may become a reason to put tracking in order.
