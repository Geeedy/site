## What document workflow automation is

Document workflow automation means moving creation, approval, signing, and storage of documents from manual operations into a system with routes, statuses, and deadline control. The modern level adds AI processing: the machine itself recognizes inbound documents, checks details, and files everything where it belongs.

## ECM, e-document exchange, and AI processing: what differs

The market mixes three different tools; that causes half of failed purchases.

| | ECM / DMS (СЭД) | E-document exchange (ЭДО) | AI processing (IDP) |
|---|---|---|---|
| What it does | Internal documents: routes, approvals, archive | Legally significant exchange with counterparties via an operator | Recognizes, checks, and routes documents |
| Example task | Approve a contract in a day, not a week | Send a UTD without paper and a courier | A scanned invoice becomes a 1C document on its own |
| Who needs it | Companies with approval volume | Anyone exchanging primary documents | Anyone whose people type data from documents |

Tools do not replace each other; they stack. A common mistake is buying a heavy ECM where an e-document operator plus AI parsing of inbound mail would have been enough.

## AI layer: what it does with documents

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/avtomatizatsiya-dokumentooborota-1.svg | Document path with AI processing]
[IMG: ../assets/images/avtomatizatsiya-dokumentooborota-1.jpg | Recognizing primary documents]

AI processing (IDP) covers the most expensive part of document workflow: manual entry and checking:

1. **Recognition.** Scans and PDFs become structured data: invoices, acts, UTDs, contracts.
2. **Checking.** Details are checked against directories, amounts against orders, counterparties against the base.
3. **Routing.** The document goes to the right approver on its own, and data to the accounting system.
4. **Human control.** Non-typical and doubtful cases go to a review queue; the machine does not post them alone.

## FAQ

**What is document workflow automation?**
Moving creation, approval, signing, and storage of documents from manual operations into a system with routes and statuses. The modern level adds AI: recognizing inbound docs, checking details, automatic routing.

**ECM, e-document exchange, and IDP: what differs?**
ECM manages internal documents and approvals; e-document exchange provides legally significant exchange with counterparties; IDP (AI processing) recognizes and checks documents, removing manual entry. Tools stack; they do not replace each other.

**Do we need ECM, or is AI processing enough?**
It depends on the pain. If the main problem is manual entry from scans, an AI layer on current systems is enough. If approvals drown and documents get lost, you need ECM. On the flow audit we calculate both with total cost of ownership.

**Which documents are automated first?**
The highest volume: inbound invoices, acts, UTDs, waybills. They have a typical structure, large volume, and a clear cost of manual processing, so the effect shows in weeks. Contracts and HR documents usually come second.

**What is the effect in numbers?**
Per an industry report, document processing cost falls 70–80%, manual-entry errors by 90%, audit and reconciliation speed up 35–40%. Per ECM reviews, typical documents complete a cycle in minutes instead of hours. Your numbers come from the pilot.

**How does AI recognize documents, and what about errors?**
The model extracts details from scans and PDFs and checks them against directories. Confident cases pass automatically; doubtful ones go to a human for review. The confidence threshold is configurable, so errors do not silently enter accounting.

**Does it integrate with 1C?**
Yes, that is a standard link: a recognized document lands in 1C with filled details; approval statuses return. We cover this in detail on the AI in 1C page.

**What about legal significance and signature?**
Documents with a qualified electronic signature are legally significant. Exchange with counterparties goes through an e-document operator. The list of mandatory electronic documents is expanding; from 01.09.2026 it includes key transport documents.

**What does it cost?**
Market guides: SaaS ECM from about 250–500 RUB per user per month, AI pilot from about 100,000–300,000 RUB, mid-market complex projects 0.7–3M RUB. We price for your flow individually. The estimate is fixed after a free flow audit.

**What are the timelines?**
An AI-processing pilot on one document type takes weeks. ECM implementation by market runs from 4 weeks for cloud systems to several months on large projects. Exact timing is visible after the flow audit.

**What about 152-FZ and security?**
Models run in your perimeter or on Russian hosting; documents do not leave to external services. Access is limited; actions are logged. 152-FZ requirements are built into the architecture at the start, not after launch.

**What do we do with the paper archive?**
Digitize selectively. Full digitization of an old archive rarely pays back, so documents are usually scanned as they are needed, and from the go-live date the entire new flow is electronic.
