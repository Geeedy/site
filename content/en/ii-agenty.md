A chatbot answers questions. An AI agent does the work: takes a request, checks data in CRM, prepares a document, assigns a manager, and writes to the client. We build agents for specific processes, connect them to your systems, and take them to production, keeping human control on critical steps.

## What an AI agent is, in plain language

An AI agent is a language-model program that independently runs a chain of actions in company systems: CRM, email, 1C, messengers. The agent gets a goal, breaks it into steps, executes them, and hands atypical cases to a human.

How it works: goal (“process the request”) → step plan → actions in systems via integrations → result check → escalate to a human if the case is atypical. The agents segment is already the largest in Russian AI software and holds [29% of the AI software market](https://www.cnews.ru/news/line/2025-12-10_58_mlrd_rubsostavit_rossijskij).

## How an agent connects to your systems

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/ii-agenty-2.svg | Agent inside the company perimeter]

The agent works where your employees already work: in CRM (Bitrix24, amoCRM), 1C, email, Telegram and WhatsApp, telephony. It takes knowledge from your base via RAG: policies, price lists, instructions. The cleaner the data, the more useful the agent. If CRM is chaos, we first [clean up the pipeline](/uslugi/vnedrenie-crm/).

## Control and security: human-in-the-loop

The agent gets minimum permissions for the task. It reads a lot but changes only what is agreed. Critical actions (payments, deletions, official replies) go to a human for confirmation. All actions are logged. Data does not leave your perimeter: we use Russian models or open models in your infrastructure, with 152-FZ in mind.

## Why 90% of agent pilots never reach production

By market estimates, most companies stall at the pilot stage: a nice demo exists, a working process does not. The reasons are the same as for [AI pilots in general](/uslugi/vnedrenie-ii/): no metric, agent not embedded in real systems, nobody owns the outcome.

We design the agent for production from day one: real integrations from the start, before/after metrics, a plan to expand permissions as accuracy rises. Our pilot is the first step of a live rollout, not a separate “look and see” project.

## How we build an agent

1. **Choose the process.** We calculate economics: hours, rate, cost of errors. We take the process with fast payback.
2. **Action map.** We spell out steps, systems, permissions, escalation points.
3. **MVP agent.** We launch with limited permissions and part of the flow.
4. **Quality control.** For the first weeks a human checks results; errors go into the knowledge base.
5. **Expand authority.** We grant more autonomy as confirmed accuracy grows.

[IMG: ../assets/images/ii-agenty-2.jpg | Building an AI agent for company processes]

**From practice.** We deliberately cut agent permissions at the start. The first week it prepares drafts; a human sends them. That is slower at the start but faster overall: the team trusts the agent, and the knowledge base grows from real edits.

## FAQ

**What is an AI agent and how does it differ from a chatbot?**
An agent takes actions in systems: creates tickets, fills CRM, prepares documents. A bot answers questions in a dialogue. A bot is a good first step, because its knowledge base and integrations are reused when the bot grows into an agent.

**What tasks can you hand to an agent?**
Repeatable tasks with clear rules: lead qualification, support replies, document processing, reports, resume screening. Not a fit: high-cost-of-error tasks with no verification path, and creative tasks without result criteria.

**SaaS agent or custom build: how to choose?**
SaaS (from roughly 990 to 15,000 RUB/month) fits typical scenarios without deep integrations. Custom build is needed when the process is specific, systems are several, and data is critical. Compare annual cost of ownership with the cost of errors from a generic solution; we will help calculate.

**How much does an AI agent cost?**
Market benchmarks: custom projects from roughly 500k RUB, PoC at large integrators roughly 1–3M RUB. We price individually. It depends on integrations, knowledge volume, and process criticality. The estimate is locked before kickoff.

**When does an agent pay back?**
Market-wide in 8–18 months with operating costs down 15–30%. The exact figure depends on routine volume: hours × employee rate minus agent cost. We calculate at audit, before you pay for development.

**How does the agent get access to our systems?**
Via API and service accounts with minimum permissions: it reads what the task needs and changes only what is agreed. Critical actions require human confirmation. Every agent action is logged.

**What if the agent makes a mistake?**
Errors are limited by permissions: the agent cannot do what is not allowed. Atypical cases it hands to a human. The first weeks every result is checked; error reviews grow the knowledge base, and accuracy rises weekly.

**What data is needed to launch?**
Process policies, examples of completed tasks, system access. Nobody has a perfect knowledge base, so we start with what exists and expand from real agent cases.

**Why do agent pilots never reach production?**
By market estimates, most pilots stay demos: no metrics, no real integrations, no owner. We design for production from the start: live systems from day one, before/after metrics, staged permission expansion.

**Does the agent run on Russian models?**
Yes: YandexGPT, GigaChat, or open models in your perimeter. Choice depends on data requirements and budget. Personal data does not leave your infrastructure; architecture is built with 152-FZ in mind.

**How many agents does a company need?**
Start with one, on the process with fast payback. A working agent with before/after numbers answers this better than strategy decks. Then add agents one process at a time, reusing the knowledge base.

**Can we start with one small process?**
Yes, that is the right way. Start with a small process with clear economics. You get a working agent, the team adapts, and you decide on scaling from your own numbers.

**“Digital employee” and AI agent: the same thing?**
Yes. “Digital employee” is the marketing name for an AI agent tied to role scenarios: digital manager, digital lawyer. Technically it is the same agent: a language model plus system integrations and permissions.
