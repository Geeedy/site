# AI in HR: the Agent Screens Applications, Humans Make Decisions

A recruiter can spend a day sorting a hundred applications while a strong candidate accepts another offer. An AI agent does the first pass in minutes: it matches applications to requirements, prepares a shortlist with rationale, and coordinates interviews. People decisions stay with humans; that is a principle, not an option. The HR agent is the fourth role in our [AI agents](/uslugi/ii-agenty/) line.

## What AI does in HR

AI in HR means applying language models to people processes: application screening, interview coordination, answers to typical employee questions, onboarding, and HR analytics. The agent takes the mechanical work; people assessment and final decisions stay with the HR team.

## The agent across HR processes

[IMG: ../assets/images/dlya-hr-1.jpg | Recruiter reviewing applications with AI]

### Application screening
The agent matches each application to vacancy requirements and returns a ranked shortlist with rationale per candidate. Market benchmarks show AI can save up to 70% of first-pass screening time, and vacancies close 35% faster.

### Interview coordination
Candidate messaging, slot alignment, reminders. The recruiter arrives at a ready interview instead of spending a day on calendar logistics.

### HR bot for employees
Leave, certificates, company policies, and other typical questions. Per Gartner guidance, a tuned HR bot closes 60–75% of such requests without HR staff.

### Onboarding
Adaptation checklists, answers to a newcomer’s typical questions, stage tracking. Per IBM data, automated onboarding raises new-hire retention by 82%.

### HR analytics
Attrition-risk forecasts, hiring-funnel analytics, plain-language questions to people data. This scenario runs on our [AI analytics](/uslugi/ii-agenty/ii-analitika/) engine.

## People decisions stay with humans

HR decisions are more sensitive than most others, so the agent is built conservatively. The machine prepares material: shortlists, summaries, drafts. Only a human can reject a candidate or change an employee status. Selection criteria are written down and checked for bias: the agent scores experience and skills, not gender, age, or surname. Every agent decision can be traced to a reason.

## Candidate personal data

CVs and HR data are processed in your perimeter or on Russian hosting under 152-FZ (Russia’s personal-data law). Agent access is limited to assigned processes; actions are logged. Candidate consent wording is configured to legal requirements.

## Pilot in 4 steps

The pilot follows the same method as the [sales agent](/uslugi/ii-agenty/dlya-prodazh/) and the other roles in the line: a small process, real data, metrics. That is part of our [AI implementation](/uslugi/vnedrenie-ii/) approach.

## FAQ

**What can AI do in HR?**
Application screening with a ranked shortlist, interview coordination, answers to typical employee questions, onboarding support, HR analytics and forecasts. The agent takes the mechanics; people decisions stay with the HR team.

**Will AI replace recruiters?**
No. The agent takes application sorting and calendar logistics; candidate assessment, interviews, and offers stay with people. In practice the team fills more vacancies with the same headcount: market benchmarks show 35% faster closing.

**What should we automate first?**
The process with the heaviest mechanical load. With active hiring that is application screening; with a large headcount, an HR bot for typical employee questions. At the audit we cost both and recommend by the numbers.

**How does screening not lose strong candidates?**
The agent ranks, it does not discard: the recruiter sees the full list with rationale, not only the top. Threshold rules are set conservatively, and in the first weeks every shortlist is human-checked, which calibrates the criteria.

**What about candidate personal data?**
Processing in your perimeter or on Russian hosting under 152-FZ; access is limited; actions are logged. Candidate consent wording is configured to legal requirements.

**How is algorithm bias controlled?**
Selection criteria are written down: experience, skills, vacancy requirements. Gender, age, and other protected attributes are not part of scoring. Every agent decision can be traced to a criterion; regular sample checks are part of support.

**Ready-made HR SaaS or an agent for our processes?**
Ready ATS products with AI work well for standard hiring. An agent is justified when you have several processes (screening + bot + onboarding), your own systems, and specific selection logic. We compare both paths honestly at the audit.

**What does it cost?**
It depends on processes and hiring volume; we price individually. Decision guide: recruiter hours on mechanics × rate + cost of open roles sitting empty. The estimate is fixed after the audit.

**When does it pay back?**
For the agent line the guide is 8–18 months; in HR usually faster with active hiring: effect comes from recruiter hours and faster vacancy closing. Exact calculation on your funnel before start.

**Do you integrate with our ATS and 1C:ZUP?**
Technically the agent connects via API to ATS, HR systems, and email. Integration options with specific systems (Huntflow, Potok, and others) we check at the audit against your stack.

**What does an employee HR bot cover?**
Typical requests: leave, certificates, sick leave, company policies, contacts. Per Gartner guidance that is 60–75% of requests to HR. Non-typical questions the bot hands to an HR specialist with context.

**Where do we start a pilot?**
With one process and a clear metric: screening on open vacancies with time-to-shortlist measured, or an HR bot with share of closed requests. The pilot takes weeks and gives numbers for a scale-up decision.
