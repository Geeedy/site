# AI for Lawyers: the Agent Drafts, the Lawyer Decides

Legal work is documents, and much of it repeats: standard contracts, checklist reviews, searching the archive, forms. An AI agent takes that routine and returns lawyers’ time to what needs judgment: risk assessment, negotiations, non-standard cases. Every agent output is checked and approved by a lawyer. That is a principle, not an option.

## What AI can do in legal work

An AI agent for lawyers is a program that does preparatory document work by rules set by your lawyers: reviews contracts against checklists, searches the document base, prepares standard forms, and watches deadlines. Decisions stay with people.

[IMG: ../assets/images/agenty-dlya-yuristov-1.jpg | Lawyer reviewing a draft prepared by an AI agent]

### First-pass contract review
The agent walks a contract against your checklist: mandatory terms, party details, deviations from the standard form, disputed wording. The lawyer gets a marked-up document with comments.

### Document-base search
Questions like “which contracts with this counterparty are active” or “where did we already approve this wording” get answers in minutes instead of manual folder hunting.

### Standard forms
The agent fills standard documents from templates. Data is pulled from your systems; the lawyer checks the result.

### Deadline control
Contract endings, response deadlines, renewals. The agent reminds in advance, not after the fact.

### Summarization
Short summaries of long documents and correspondence: substance, parties, obligations, risks. That speeds immersion into a matter.

## What the agent does not do

We state applicability limits plainly because we work with a professional audience. The agent does not give legal opinions, does not assess dispute prospects, does not sign or send documents without a human, and does not replace a lawyer in negotiations or court. Responsibility for decisions rests with the lawyer; the agent prepares material for the decision.

## How confidentiality is ensured

For legal work this is question number one. Models run inside the company perimeter or on Russian hosting; documents do not leave to external services. Agent access is limited to assigned folders and document types; every action is logged. If company data is still scattered across folders and systems, we help [put data in order](/uslugi/vnedrenie-crm/) before the pilot. Requirements of 152-FZ (Russia’s personal-data law) and trade-secret rules are built into the architecture from the start.

## FAQ

**What can AI do in legal work?**
First-pass contract review against checklists, document-base search, filling standard forms, deadline control, summarizing long documents. All of this is preparatory work that a lawyer then checks and approves.

**Can the agent review contracts?**
Yes, as a first pass and by rules your lawyers set: mandatory terms, deviations from the standard form, disputed wording. The agent marks up the document and hands it to the lawyer; final assessment and decision stay with the human.

**Who is responsible for agent errors?**
Responsibility for decisions rests with the lawyer, and the process is built for that: the agent drafts, the lawyer checks and approves. In the first weeks every result is reviewed; edits enrich the knowledge base; accuracy grows while control remains.

**How is confidentiality ensured?**
Models run in the company perimeter or on Russian hosting; documents do not leave to external services. Access is limited to assigned folders; actions are logged. Secrecy rules and 152-FZ are built into the architecture from the start.

**Does it work with the Russian legal base?**
The agent works with your documents and checklists: contracts, forms, policies. Integrations with reference systems we check individually for your perimeter. The agent does not form a legal position; that is the lawyer’s job.

**How many hours does it save?**
Per industry data, document processing gets 70–80% cheaper and audit speeds up 35–40%. In practice a first-pass review of a standard contract takes the agent minutes versus half an hour or more for a specialist. Your numbers come from the pilot.

**Will the agent replace junior lawyers?**
The agent takes the mechanical part of their work: standard reviews, search, forms. Junior lawyers move faster to substantive tasks: risk analysis and non-standard cases. The team handles a larger volume with the same headcount.

**What does it cost?**
It depends on document types, base volume, and perimeter requirements; we price individually. For an estimate, count hours on standard reviews per month × lawyer rate. A pilot on one contract type gives your own numbers before large spend.

**Does it integrate with ConsultantPlus or Garant?**
Integration with reference systems we check against your perimeter and licenses. The base scenario runs on your documents and needs no external systems: checklists, contract base, templates.

**Where do we start a pilot?**
With one high-volume contract type and a formalized review checklist. Then the agent prepares drafts, the lawyer checks them, and hours before and after are measured. We expand only after the numbers.
