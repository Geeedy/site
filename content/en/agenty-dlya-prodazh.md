Managers burn hours on CRM updates, reminder emails, and sorting inbound requests while leads go cold. An AI agent takes that routine: it qualifies inquiries in minutes, keeps deal cards current, writes call summaries, and stops deals from stalling. ## What AI does in sales

An AI sales agent is a program that runs routine funnel operations in your CRM: receiving and qualifying leads, filling cards, reminders, and drafting standard emails and proposals. Industry effects are documented: [AI scoring lifts win rate by up to +76%, personalization adds 15–30% to conversion, and manager productivity rises 30–50%](https://airassvet.ru/articles/effektivnost-iskusstvennogo-intellekta-v-rossiyskom-biznese-2025-2026-analiticheskiy-otchet-o-7-klyuchevyh-stsenariyah-metrikah-roi-i-strategiyah-vnedreniya).

## The agent across funnel stages

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/agenty-dlya-prodazh-2.svg | Agent in the sales funnel]
[IMG: ../assets/images/agenty-dlya-prodazh-1.jpg | Sales team: agent qualifies leads]

### Inbound lead
The agent replies first, in minutes, not “when a manager is free.” It asks qualifying questions and creates a deal with source and context.

### Qualification
It finds out what is needed, when, and with what budget. The card is filled before the manager picks up the lead.

### Working the deal
It writes call summaries into the card, drafts emails and proposals from templates, and reminds about agreements.

### Watching stalled deals
The agent spots deals with no movement and no next step. The manager gets a task; the sales lead gets a summary.

### Repeat sales
Clients with no purchases over a period enter a reactivation list, with a reason and history.

## How lead scoring works

No magic: the agent compares a new lead with your deal history by source, company size, behavior, and answers to questions. The output is a deal-probability score and a priority in the manager queue. Hot leads are handled first; cold ones do not eat team time. Scoring gets more accurate as CRM history grows, and a pilot shows the exact figure for your database.

## What we need from your CRM

The agent sits on top of CRM, and data quality decides outcomes: in market surveys, [52% of companies name data quality as the main AI-project problem](https://ict.moscow/analytics/v-krupnykh-kompaniiakh-do-stadii-vnedreniia-doshli-do-10-pilotnykh-proektov-po-ii/). ## The agent listens to calls: is that legal?

Recording calls for quality control is lawful when parties are notified (“this call may be recorded”). The agent works with the same recordings a supervisor would, only faster: transcript, summary, next step. We configure notice wording and personal-data processing to the rules.

## FAQ

**What does the agent do in the sales team?**
It replies first to inbound contacts, qualifies leads, fills cards, writes call summaries, drafts emails and proposals, watches stalled deals, and builds reactivation lists. Managers get time for negotiations.

**Will the agent replace managers?**
No. The agent takes routine work; negotiations and relationships stay with people. In practice a team with an agent handles more leads with the same headcount, and productivity rises 30–50% per industry data.

**What do we need from our CRM?**
Deals must live in the system, not in notebooks, because the agent works with CRM data. If tracking is chaotic, we start with CRM implementation or cleanup; otherwise the agent would automate chaos.

**How does lead scoring work?**
The agent compares a new lead with your deal history: source, profile, behavior, answers. The output is deal probability and priority. Hot leads are handled first. Per industry reports, scoring lifts win rate by up to +76%.

**What does a sales agent cost?**
It depends on CRM, channels, and number of scenarios; we price individually. As a guide, count manager hours on routine work and multiply by the hourly rate. The estimate is fixed after the funnel audit.

**When does it pay back?**
By market benchmarks, agent projects pay back in 8–18 months; in sales usually faster because the effect shows in closed deals. We calculate for your funnel (routine hours, rate, conversion) on a free audit.

**The agent listens to calls: is that legal?**
Yes, with notice that the call is recorded; that is standard quality-control practice. The agent processes the same recordings faster than a person. Notice wording and personal-data handling are configured to legal requirements.

**Does it work with Bitrix24 and amoCRM?**
Yes, with both platforms: reading and filling cards, tasks, scoring. We also connect telephony and messengers. If you have no CRM yet, we help choose and implement one for your team.

**How accurate is scoring?**
It depends on the volume and quality of deal history in your CRM, so promising a percentage before a pilot would be dishonest. In the pilot the agent scores leads in parallel with managers, and the comparison shows accuracy on your data.

**Where do we start if CRM is a mess?**
With CRM order: database cleanup, rules, and logging discipline. That is a separate project of weeks, after which the agent starts delivering value. Honest sequence: foundation first, then AI.
