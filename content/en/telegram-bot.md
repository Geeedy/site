## What a Telegram bot for business is

A Telegram bot for business is a program account in Telegram that answers clients, takes leads and payments, handles booking, and sends broadcasts to subscribers. The bot works around the clock where your audience already spends time, and sends every dialog to CRM.

## Telegram Business vs a bot: what differs

Telegram Business is a mode of a regular account with business features: greetings, quick replies, working hours. A bot is a separate program with its own logic: scenarios, knowledge base, payments, integrations. Do not confuse them: Telegram Business is set up in an evening and helps personal sales; a bot automates volume. Since 2025 Telegram Business officially lets you connect bots to inbound messages on a business account, so the tools work together.

## What the bot does for business

## What about WhatsApp in Russia

Honest answer: the official WhatsApp Business API is available to Russian companies through intermediary integrators, with pre-moderation of template messages and a subscription from about 5,000–10,000 RUB per month per number plus pay-per-dialog. We connect WhatsApp as an extra channel where the audience lives there, but in Russia it is usually better to start with Telegram: cheaper, more stable, and without limits on proactive messages to bot subscribers.

## Development stages

1. **Scenarios.** What the bot does, where it escalates to a human, what data it collects.
2. **Knowledge base and integrations.** Price lists and policies for AI answers, CRM and payment links.
3. **Pilot.** The bot runs on part of the audience; you read the dialogs.
4. **Launch and retraining.** Broadcasts, analytics, weekly knowledge-base expansion.

## Broadcasts without spam

Telegram broadcasts work only for bot subscribers: people who pressed Start themselves. We do not do bought lists or spam broadcasts, because that means a bot ban and brand damage. The right mechanic looks different: a useful bot gathers subscribers, segments by interest, and sends what the person agreed to receive.

## FAQ

**What can a Telegram bot for business do?**
Take leads and handle booking, answer knowledge-base questions around the clock, accept payment, send subscriber broadcasts, and automate internal processes. Every dialog lands in CRM with history.

**How does Telegram Business differ from a bot?**
Telegram Business is an account mode with greetings and quick replies, a helper for personal sales. A bot is a separate program with logic, a knowledge base, and integrations. Since 2025 they work together: a bot connects to business-account messages.

**Ready-made bot, builder, or custom development?**
Ready-made bots solve personal tasks; builders assemble simple scenarios. Custom development is justified when there is a lead flow, you need CRM, payments, or AI answers from your knowledge base. Describe the task and we will say honestly which path is yours.

**What does it cost to build a Telegram bot?**
By market: simple bot 20,000–50,000 RUB, medium with database and CRM 50,000–150,000 RUB, complex with AI and payments from 150,000 RUB. We price for your scenarios individually. The estimate is fixed before start.

**What are the timelines?**
A simple bot takes 3–10 days, medium 2–6 weeks, complex with AI and integrations 1–3 months. A pilot on part of the audience lets you launch earlier and calibrate on live dialogs.

**How does an AI bot answer from our knowledge base?**
We gather price lists, policies, and FAQs into a base, and the bot answers from it in free text, not buttons. Non-typical questions go to an operator with context. The base is shared with the website bot and voice channel.

**How do leads from the bot get into CRM?**
Automatically: the dialog creates a deal with chat history, source, and status. The manager continues from the CRM card. Telegram Business officially supports linking business accounts to external systems.

**Can we accept payment in the bot?**
Yes, via connected payment services: the client chooses a product or service and pays without leaving Telegram. Setup depends on your payment provider and product type.

**What about WhatsApp bots in Russia?**
The official API is available through integrators: subscription from about 5,000–10,000 RUB/month per number; message templates go through pre-moderation. We connect it as an extra channel, but starting with Telegram is usually better value.

**How do we broadcast without hitting spam?**
Broadcast only to bot subscribers, segment by interest, and offer easy unsubscribe. Bought lists lead to a bot ban. A proper bot gathers subscribers with value: status updates, booking, answers.

**Can one bot cover the website and Telegram?**
Yes, that is our standard: a shared knowledge base works across channels. The website widget and Telegram bot answer the same way, and leads land in one CRM funnel.

**What about personal data (152-FZ)?**
Dialogs and client data are processed in your perimeter or on Russian hosting. Consents for processing and broadcasts are built into bot scenarios. Access and logs stay under your control.
