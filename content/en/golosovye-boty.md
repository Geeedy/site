## How a voice bot differs from an IVR menu

A voice bot is AI that understands live speech and answers from the company knowledge base, instead of forcing the client to listen to “press 1, press 2.” The client speaks normally, for example “move my appointment to Friday,” and the bot acts: finds the booking, offers a time, confirms, and writes the result to CRM.

An IVR menu routes button presses. A bot runs a dialogue and solves the task. That is the whole difference.

## How it works

## Is outbound dialing legal

Yes, when rules are followed: dialing runs on a client base that consented to communications, with company introduction and an opt-out path. We account for advertising-call and personal-data requirements when configuring scenarios. We do not do grey lists or spam dialing, because that risks your brand and number.

## FAQ

**How does a voice bot differ from an IVR menu?**
IVR routes button presses; a bot understands live speech and solves the task: reschedules, files a request, answers a question. The client speaks normally and gets a result, not a wander through menus.

**How much does a voice-bot minute cost?**
The market model is process setup plus pay-per-minute talk. We calculate rates individually for your scenarios and minute volume. For the decision, compare with the full cost of an operator minute: rate, taxes, training, turnover. We will run the calculation on your numbers for free.

**Does the robot sound like a human?**
Modern voices are natural, and clients often do not immediately realize they are talking to a bot. We do not hide the bot where ethics or law matter: the bot introduces itself, and complex conversations are handled by people.

**What if the client asks for a live operator?**
The bot transfers without argument, with the recording and context. The “human on first request” rule is built into every scenario, because the bot’s job is to unload the line, not annoy clients.

**Is auto-dialing legal?**
Yes, on a client base with consent to communications, with company introduction and an opt-out. Advertising-call and personal-data requirements are built into scenarios. We do not work with grey lists.

**How fast can we launch?**
A typical scenario like appointment confirmation usually takes from two weeks. Most time goes to the knowledge base and telephony/CRM integration. A pilot on part of calls speeds the start.

**How does the bot integrate with telephony and CRM?**
It connects to your PBX or cloud telephony, and writes every call result to CRM: topic, outcome, recording. If there is no CRM yet, we start with a [request tracking system](/uslugi/vnedrenie-crm/); otherwise call results have nowhere to land.

**Does the bot share one knowledge base with the chatbot?**
Yes. Price lists, policies, and answers are set once and work in text and voice. An update instantly reaches all channels. Voice connects as a module to an existing chatbot.

**Which calls should not go to the bot?**
Conflicts, complex consultations, and negotiations need a human. The bot takes high-volume and typical ones: confirmations, bookings, status checks, first-pass qualification. We draw the line on the pilot from recordings of real conversations.

**What does the bot do with missed calls?**
There are none, because the bot answers instantly at any time. If a client called after hours with an atypical question, the bot logs a request in CRM, and in the morning the manager calls back first.
