# Bitrix24 Implementation: Staged So It Sticks

Bitrix24 is Russia’s most common CRM: per J'son & Partners, [about half of companies name it as primary](https://www.kt-team.ru/blog/crm-market-russia-2025-bitrix24-adoption-trends-digitalization). The platform can do almost everything, from CRM and tasks to documents and a portal, and that is exactly its main trap. We implement Bitrix24 in stages: sales first, the rest as the team adapts.

## Cloud or on-premise box

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/bitrix24-1.svg | Bitrix24: cloud or on-premise]

| | Cloud | On-premise box |
|---|---|---|
| Start | 1–4 weeks | Longer: server, install |
| Payment | Subscription | License + infrastructure |
| Customization | Within the plan | Any |
| Data | In Bitrix24 cloud | In your perimeter |

Most SMB companies have enough cloud: [a typical launch takes 1–4 weeks](https://elma365.com/ru/articles/crm-systema-polnyi-algorytm-vnedrenya/). The box is justified with strict data requirements or non-standard customizations.

## What we set up

[IMG: ../assets/images/bitrix24-1.jpg | Setting up pipelines and permissions in Bitrix24]

CRM always comes first: pipelines for your stages, fields and cards, role permissions, auto-tasks and robots, manager reports. That is the core that pays for implementation: requests are logged, deals move, numbers are visible.

## Integrations: website, telephony, 1C, messengers

[Website](/uslugi/razrabotka-saitov/) forms create leads with source tags. Telephony writes calls into the card. Telegram and WhatsApp gather in the platform contact center. 1C syncs invoices and payments. Each integration closes a point where requests used to get lost.

## Why turning everything on at once is a mistake

Per industry reviews, [30–50% of CRM implementations miss their goals](https://www.e-xecutive.ru/management/itforbusiness/1988139-5-prichin-provalov-vo-vnedrenii-crm), and trying to launch all modules at once remains a typical cause. The team drowns in new interfaces, sabotages the system, and goes back to Excel.

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/bitrix24-2.svg | Bitrix24 implementation without chaos: staged]

Our method is built differently: CRM and sales go first, two to three weeks go to adaptation with discipline control, then tasks and projects connect, then documents and portal. Each next module turns on when the previous one has become habit.

## AI on top of Bitrix24

The platform’s built-in AI covers basic scenarios. On top we add [AI agents](/uslugi/ii-agenty/) for your processes: lead qualification, call summarization into cards, stalled-deal control. Per an industry report, [AI scoring lifts win rate by up to +76%](https://airassvet.ru/articles/effektivnost-iskusstvennogo-intellekta-v-rossiyskom-biznese-2025-2026-analiticheskiy-otchet-o-7-klyuchevyh-stsenariyah-metrikah-roi-i-strategiyah-vnedreniya).

## Training and adoption

[IMG: ../assets/images/bitrix24-2.jpg | Training the team to work in Bitrix24]

We train on your company’s live deals, not demo examples. The first weeks we control discipline together with the manager, because CRM dies not from settings but from manager silence when people “forget” to log. Policies defining what, who, and when must enter are part of the project.

## When Bitrix24 is overkill

Honestly: if the task is limited to a sales team of 2–20 people with short deals, [amoCRM](/uslugi/vnedrenie-crm/amocrm/) will launch faster and stick more easily. Bitrix24 is strong when you need whole-company processes. We implement both platforms, and [vendor-neutral comparison](/uslugi/vnedrenie-crm/) is part of the audit.

## FAQ

**How much does Bitrix24 implementation cost?**
Market ranges: basic roughly 30–80k RUB, standard roughly 100–300k RUB, advanced with integrations roughly 400–900k RUB. We calculate implementation price individually. Platform licenses are paid separately, to the vendor. The estimate locks after the audit.

**Cloud or on-premise: what to choose?**
Cloud gives a fast start in 1–4 weeks, a subscription, and automatic updates; that is most SMBs’ choice. The box keeps data in your perimeter and allows any customization; it is for strict security requirements. At audit we will calculate cost of ownership for both.

**How long does launch take?**
Cloud CRM with basic integrations launches in 1–4 weeks. A full contour with 1C, telephony, and training takes 1.5–3 months. The staged method delivers working sales already in the first weeks.

**Licenses: whose and how much?**
Licenses are issued to you and paid to Bitrix24; plans depend on user count and modules. Current vendor prices we show at audit. Our work is paid separately from licenses.

**What do you set up specifically?**
Pipelines for your stages, fields and cards, role permissions, robots and auto-tasks, manager reports, integrations with website, telephony, 1C, and messengers, work policies, and team training.

**Do you integrate with 1C?**
Yes. Invoices, payments, shipments, and stock sync. The manager sees payment status in the deal card without calling accounting. Sync depth locks at audit.

**Is migration from amoCRM or Excel possible?**
Yes. Contacts, companies, and deal history we transfer with relationships preserved and duplicates cleaned. The migration plan is agreed before kickoff, because a dirty base in a new system reproduces the old chaos.

**How do you get employees used to Bitrix24?**
By staged module rollout, training on live deals, policies, and first-weeks control with the manager. Automation helps too: when the system sets tasks itself, resistance drops.

**What about AI in Bitrix24?**
The platform’s built-in AI covers basic scenarios; on top we add agents for your processes: lead qualification, call summaries, stalled-deal control. Lead scoring per industry data lifts win rate by up to +76%.

**When is Bitrix24 overkill?**
When you only need a sales team of 2–20 people with short deals; there amoCRM will launch faster. Bitrix24 is justified with tasks, documents, and a portal for the whole company. We recommend honestly, because we implement both platforms.
