# Website Chatbot: the Visitor Gets an Answer, You Get a Lead

A visitor arrives with a question. If there is no answer in the first minutes, they leave to search elsewhere, and money for that click is wasted. An AI chatbot replies in seconds from the company knowledge base, qualifies the visitor, and sends a lead to CRM with the dialog history. This is the site-focused version of our [AI chatbots](/uslugi/chat-boty/), and before a contract we show a demo on your questions.

## What a website chatbot is

A website chatbot is a widget that answers visitors, collects contacts, and passes leads to managers. Button bots walk menus; an AI bot understands free-text questions and answers from the company knowledge base: price lists, policies, service descriptions.

## What the bot does on the site

1. **Answers questions** about services, prices, and terms, around the clock and in seconds.
2. **Collects and qualifies leads.** Asks clarifying questions and hands the manager a warm contact.
3. **Books a consultation** or site visit, synced with the calendar.
4. **Catches overnight leads.** In the morning the manager sees a queue of qualified dialogs, not emptiness.
5. **Offloads support.** Per Markswebb research, bots close [66% of typical customer requests](https://www.kommersant.ru/doc/4899688).

## Why reply speed decides

[INFOGRAPHIC: ../assets/infographics/bot-na-sait-1.svg | Reply speed and lead probability]
[IMG: ../assets/images/bot-na-sait-1.jpg | Website visitor chatting from a phone]

Per chat-service research, a reply within a minute raises lead probability 2–3× versus waiting more than five minutes, and with a delay over 15 minutes only 5–10% of those who started a dialog reach a lead. A manager physically cannot reply in a minute to everyone always; a bot can. Across aggregated agency cases, adding a chat with a bot gives +10–30% to site-to-lead conversion.

## How an AI bot differs from a button bot

A button bot follows a rigid “press 1” tree, and one step aside leaves the client stuck. An AI bot understands free wording, typos, and conversational language, answers from the knowledge base, and hands non-typical cases to an operator with context. A full comparison with a table is on the [AI chatbots](/uslugi/chat-boty/) page.

## Lead path: from dialog to CRM

[IMG: ../assets/images/bot-na-sait-2.jpg | Manager receives a qualified lead from chat]

Every dialog becomes a deal in [CRM](/uslugi/vnedrenie-crm/): with source, UTM tag, topic, and full chat history. The bot asks qualifying questions by your script, so the manager starts with context, not “what did you want?” Leadership sees in the funnel how many leads the site brought and which reached money.

## How we launch

1. **Knowledge base.** We gather price lists, policies, and FAQs. That is 80% of bot quality.
2. **Qualification and escalation scenarios.** What the bot asks and when it hands off to an operator.
3. **Pilot on part of traffic.** We watch real dialogs and calibrate answers.
4. **Learning from dialogs.** Every week we review hard cases and expand the knowledge base.

## FAQ

**What can a website chatbot do?**
Answer knowledge-base questions in seconds, collect and qualify leads, book services, catch overnight leads, and offload support: by market figures bots close 66% of typical requests. All of that around the clock.

**How does an AI bot differ from a button widget?**
A button bot follows a rigid menu and loses the client at one step aside. An AI bot understands free wording and typos, answers from your knowledge base, and hands non-typical cases to an operator with full dialog context.

**When is free Jivo enough, and when do we need a build?**
If an operator is free in business hours and questions are rare, a free widget is enough. An AI bot is justified when questions are many, leads arrive overnight, and each is worth money. On the demo we say honestly which path is yours.

**What does an AI website bot cost?**
Widgets cost from 0 to about 2,000 RUB/month per service list prices as of July 2026. A custom AI bot with knowledge base and CRM integration we estimate individually. The estimate is fixed after scoping; the demo is free.

**How much does a bot raise conversion?**
Across aggregated agency cases, chat with a bot gives +10–30% to lead conversion. The key mechanism is speed: a reply within a minute raises lead chance 2–3×. Your numbers come from a pilot on part of traffic.

**How do leads get into CRM?**
Automatically: the dialog creates a deal with source, UTM tag, and chat history. The bot qualifies by your questions and assigns an owner. The manager continues from CRM with full context.

**What does the bot answer at night and on weekends?**
The same as by day: full knowledge-base answers, booking, lead intake. Questions that need a human are logged with a promise of a business-hours reply, and the lead is already in CRM.

**How fast does it launch?**
Most time goes to the knowledge base. With ready price lists and policies, launch usually takes from two weeks. A pilot on part of traffic lets you start earlier and retrain the bot on live dialogs.

**What do you need from us to launch?**
Price lists, service descriptions, policies, and frequent questions with answers. If materials are scattered, we build the knowledge base together in interviews with your team. Site access is needed only to install the widget.

**Can we put a bot on Tilda or an existing site?**
Yes, the widget connects to any site without a rebuild, including website builders. If the site is technically outdated, we say so separately, but that usually does not block the bot.

**How does the bot learn from dialogs?**
Weekly we review dialogs where the bot failed and expand the knowledge base. Accuracy grows week by week; that is part of support, not sold separately.

**Where is dialog data stored?**
In your perimeter or on Russian hosting; architecture is chosen with 152-FZ in mind. Personal data is not sent to external services without your decision.
