## Why a business needs CRM

Sales channels keep multiplying: online commerce grew [32% year over year](https://myseldon.com/ru/news/index/340350053), and requests are scattered across messengers, email, and phone. Without one system, part of the inquiries simply never reach a manager in time.

CRM solves this with mechanics, not slogans. Every inquiry from any channel automatically becomes a deal with an owner and a deadline; client history lives in the card, not in a departed manager’s head; and the pipeline shows where sales stall.

## Why 30–50% of CRM implementations fail

According to industry reviews, [30–50% of CRM projects miss their goals](https://www.e-xecutive.ru/management/itforbusiness/1988139-5-prichin-provalov-vo-vnedrenii-crm). The reasons repeat: rolled out without measurable goals, processes were not described, data was dirty, managers sabotaged and went back to Excel, the project had no owner.

Our safeguards match each reason: goals and metrics lock at audit, processes are described before setup, the database is cleaned at migration, training runs on live deals, and for the first weeks we control discipline together with the manager. CRM dies not from bad setup but from manager silence in the first two weeks, so control is part of the project, not an add-on.

## How implementation works

1. **Audit.** Channels, processes, loss points. Takes 1–2 weeks.
2. **Setup.** Pipelines, fields, permissions, automation.
3. **Migration.** We move the base from Excel or an old CRM, with deduplication.
4. **Policies.** We lock what a manager must enter and when.
5. **Training.** On real deals, not slides.
6. **First-weeks control.** Discipline and fine-tuning from actual scenarios.

**From practice.** The fastest way to kill CRM is to turn on every module at once. We turn sales on first; the rest as the team adapts. The typical “everything at once” mistake tops the list of failure causes.

## FAQ

**Bitrix24, amoCRM, or 1C:CRM: what to choose?**
amoCRM gives sales team speed and a fast start; Bitrix24 covers whole-company processes with tasks and a portal; 1C:CRM fits business that lives in 1C. Per J'son & Partners, about half of companies use Bitrix24, but choose by tasks. On the audit we give a recommendation with rationale.

**How much does CRM implementation cost?**
Market ranges: basic roughly 30–80k RUB, standard roughly 100–300k RUB, advanced roughly 400–900k RUB, enterprise from roughly 1M RUB. Our price is calculated individually after the audit. Platform licenses are paid to the vendor separately.

**How long does implementation take?**
Cloud platforms deliver a working pipeline in 1–4 weeks. Basic implementation takes 3–8 weeks; for mid-size business 1.5–3 months. Data migration and 1C integrations add time; the exact timeline is clear after the audit.

**Why do CRM implementations fail, and what do you do differently?**
Market-wide 30–50% of projects miss goals: no measurable goals, processes not described, dirty data, manager sabotage, no owner. Our safeguards: goals at audit, described processes, database cleanup, training on live deals, first-weeks control.

**Will you migrate data from Excel or an old CRM?**
Yes: contacts, companies, deal history with relationships preserved. We clean duplicates at migration, because a dirty base in a new CRM reproduces the old chaos. The migration plan is agreed before kickoff.

**How do you get managers to work in CRM?**
You need a policy, training on real deals, and first-weeks control. Without any of the three, implementation dies. Automation helps: when CRM sets tasks and writes call summaries itself, resistance drops noticeably.

**What is end-to-end analytics?**
Reporting that links channel spend to deal revenue: how much a lead from each source cost and how much money it brought. It shows real channel payback instead of gut feel and click stats.

**How does AI strengthen CRM?**
AI writes call summaries into cards, scores leads by deal probability, suggests the next step. Per an industry report, scoring lifts win rate by up to +76%. It all starts with clean data: CRM first, then AI.

**Do you integrate with 1C?**
Yes. Invoices, payments, shipments, and stock sync between CRM and 1C. The manager sees payment status in the card; accounting does not answer “did the client pay?” ten times a day.

**What happens after launch?**
The first weeks we control discipline and fine-tune the system from real scenarios. Then we work under a support agreement. All access and settings are yours; there is no dependency on us.

**Do we need CRM if we have three managers?**
If requests come from two or more channels, yes. Three managers lose inquiries the same way thirty do; it is just less visible. For a small team we set a light configuration, and it pays back in response speed.

**Licenses: whose and how much?**
Licenses are always issued to you and paid to the vendor. Plans depend on user count and features; current prices we show at audit. Our work (implementation, integrations, training) is paid separately from licenses.
