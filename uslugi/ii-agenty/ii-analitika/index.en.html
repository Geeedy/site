AI Analytics: Ask Your Data in Plain Language

Company numbers sit in CRM, 1C, Yandex Metrica, and a dozen spreadsheets, while a simple question gets answered “by Friday.” An AI analyst answers in minutes: you ask in plain language, the agent finds data in your systems and returns an answer with figures and a chart. It is an analyst who does not resign, get sick, or tire of the same questions.

Demo on your data Ask a question

10 questions you will ask the agent on day one

  1. How many leads did the website bring this month, and what is conversion to deal?
  2. What is revenue by each line versus last quarter?
  3. Which managers closed the most deals, and whose funnel is stuck?
  4. Which products leave the warehouse faster than we restock them?
  5. How much did a lead cost from each ad channel?
  6. Which clients have not bought in more than six months?
  7. Where are we losing margin, by which categories and why?
  8. What is the sales forecast for next month?
  9. Which invoices have been unpaid for more than 30 days?
  10. What changed this week that I should know about?

If you currently answer at least three of these with exports and manual pivots, the agent will pay back quickly.

What an AI analyst can do

An AI analyst is an agent connected to company data. It understands a plain-language question, gathers data from systems, and returns an answer with figures, a table, or a chart.

Answers questions

It takes any question about the data, without SQL, without exports, without waiting for an analyst.

Builds scheduled reports

The Monday sales summary arrives on its own, assembled from CRM, 1C, and Metrica.

Forecasts

It builds sales and demand forecasts. ML forecasts are 10–30% more accurate than traditional methods.

Spots anomalies

A sharp rise in refusals, a conversion drop, atypical spend. The agent reports on its own, without waiting for a question.

Unifies sources

One answer from several systems: ads from Metrica, deals from CRM, payments from 1C. For inbound-lead assessment there is a related tool, lead scoring.

How it differs from BI dashboards

A dashboard answers the questions guessed at setup time. One step aside and you need a rebuild. The agent answers any question about connected data: today about conversion by manager, tomorrow about margin by category. Dashboards are not cancelled; the agent complements them, covering the “tail” of non-standard questions that dashboards are not rebuilt for.

Which data connects

CRM (Bitrix24, amoCRM), 1C, Yandex Metrica, ad accounts, Excel and Google Sheets. Answer quality depends on data quality: in surveys, 52% of companies name data as the main AI-project problem. If tracking is chaotic, start with CRM implementation. The agent will honestly show data gaps already on the demo.

Accuracy in numbers

Forecasts are 10–30% more accurate; management decisions are made 20–40% faster. Not because AI is smarter than people, but because data is gathered in minutes, not by Friday.

Launch in 4 steps

  1. Connect sources. CRM, 1C, Metrica, spreadsheets via API, read-only.
  2. Check data. The agent shows where data conflicts, and that alone is often valuable.
  3. First questions. The team asks real questions; we calibrate answers.
  4. Reports and anomalies. We set up regular summaries and alerts.

What it costs

It depends on the number of sources and data complexity, so we price individually. For comparison, take a junior analyst’s salary plus taxes, vacation, and training; the agent answers instantly and for the whole team at once. We run a demo on your data before a contract.

Why us
