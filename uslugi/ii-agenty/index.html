AI Agents: Digital Employees That Run Processes, Not Just Answer Questions

A chatbot answers questions. An AI agent does the work: takes a request, checks data in CRM, prepares a document, assigns a manager, and writes to the client. We build agents for specific processes, connect them to your systems, and take them to production, keeping human control on critical steps.

What an AI agent is, in plain language

An AI agent is a language-model program that independently runs a chain of actions in company systems: CRM, email, 1C, messengers. The agent gets a goal, breaks it into steps, executes them, and hands atypical cases to a human.

How it works: goal (“process the request”) → step plan → actions in systems via integrations → result check → escalate to a human if the case is atypical. The agents segment is already the largest in Russian AI software and holds 29% of the AI software market.

How an agent connects to your systems

The agent works where your employees already work: in CRM (Bitrix24, amoCRM), 1C, email, Telegram and WhatsApp, telephony. It takes knowledge from your base via RAG: policies, price lists, instructions. The cleaner the data, the more useful the agent. If CRM is chaos, we first clean up the pipeline.

Control and security: human-in-the-loop

The agent gets minimum permissions for the task. It reads a lot but changes only what is agreed. Critical actions (payments, deletions, official replies) go to a human for confirmation. All actions are logged. Data does not leave your perimeter: we use Russian models or open models in your infrastructure, with 152-FZ in mind.

Why 90% of agent pilots never reach production

By market estimates, most companies stall at the pilot stage: a nice demo exists, a working process does not. The reasons are the same as for AI pilots in general: no metric, agent not embedded in real systems, nobody owns the outcome.

We design the agent for production from day one: real integrations from the start, before/after metrics, a plan to expand permissions as accuracy rises. Our pilot is the first step of a live rollout, not a separate “look and see” project.

How we build an agent

  1. Choose the process. We calculate economics: hours, rate, cost of errors. We take the process with fast payback.
  2. Action map. We spell out steps, systems, permissions, escalation points.
  3. MVP agent. We launch with limited permissions and part of the flow.
  4. Quality control. For the first weeks a human checks results; errors go into the knowledge base.
  5. Expand authority. We grant more autonomy as confirmed accuracy grows.

From practice. We deliberately cut agent permissions at the start. The first week it prepares drafts; a human sends them. That is slower at the start but faster overall: the team trusts the agent, and the knowledge base grows from real edits.

When an agent pays back

Market-wide, agent projects pay back in 8–18 months, cutting operating costs 15–30% and saving up to 40–50% of payroll cost on routine roles. Your number is simple: routine volume in hours × employee rate, minus agent and support cost. We run this calculation at audit, before work starts.
