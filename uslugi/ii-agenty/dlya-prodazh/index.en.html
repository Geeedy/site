AI Sales Agent: Managers Sell, the Agent Handles the Routine

Managers burn hours on CRM updates, reminder emails, and sorting inbound requests while leads go cold. An AI agent takes that routine: it qualifies inquiries in minutes, keeps deal cards current, writes call summaries, and stops deals from stalling. People stay where they are irreplaceable: in negotiations.

Funnel audit Ask a question

What AI does in sales

An AI sales agent is a program that runs routine funnel operations in your CRM: receiving and qualifying leads, filling cards, reminders, and drafting standard emails and proposals. Industry effects are documented: AI scoring lifts win rate by up to +76%, personalization adds 15–30% to conversion, and manager productivity rises 30–50%.

The agent across funnel stages

Inbound lead

The agent replies first, in minutes, not “when a manager is free.” It asks qualifying questions and creates a deal with source and context.

Qualification

It finds out what is needed, when, and with what budget. The card is filled before the manager picks up the lead.

Working the deal

It writes call summaries into the card, drafts emails and proposals from templates, and reminds about agreements.

Watching stalled deals

The agent spots deals with no movement and no next step. The manager gets a task; the sales lead gets a summary.

Repeat sales

Clients with no purchases over a period enter a reactivation list, with a reason and history.

How lead scoring works

No magic: the agent compares a new lead with your deal history by source, company size, behavior, and answers to questions. The output is a deal-probability score and a priority in the manager queue. Hot leads are handled first; cold ones do not eat team time. Scoring gets more accurate as CRM history grows, and a pilot shows the exact figure for your database.

What we need from your CRM

The agent sits on top of CRM, and data quality decides outcomes: in market surveys, 52% of companies name data quality as the main AI-project problem. If deals live “in people’s heads,” start with putting sales in order; that is the foundation. We work with Bitrix24 and amoCRM.

The agent listens to calls: is that legal?

Recording calls for quality control is lawful when parties are notified (“this call may be recorded”). The agent works with the same recordings a supervisor would, only faster: transcript, summary, next step. We configure notice wording and personal-data processing to the rules.

Launch in 4 steps

  1. Funnel audit. Stages, conversions, where leads are lost.
  2. First scenario. Usually inbound qualification, which gives a fast effect.
  3. Pilot. The agent runs on part of the flow; managers check results.
  4. Expansion. Scoring, stalled-deal control, and reactivation are added as trust grows.

What it costs and when it pays back

Price depends on CRM, channels, and scenarios, so we calculate it for your funnel. Agent projects pay back in 8–18 months by market benchmarks; in sales the figure is usually closer to the lower end because the effect shows in closed deals, not only in hours saved. We run the numbers on your data at the audit.
