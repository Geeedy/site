AI in HR: the Agent Screens Applications, Humans Make Decisions

A recruiter can spend a day sorting a hundred applications while a strong candidate accepts another offer. An AI agent does the first pass in minutes: it matches applications to requirements, prepares a shortlist with rationale, and coordinates interviews. People decisions stay with humans; that is a principle, not an option. The HR agent is the fourth role in our AI agents line.

Pilot on one HR process Ask a question

What AI does in HR

AI in HR means applying language models to people processes: application screening, interview coordination, answers to typical employee questions, onboarding, and HR analytics. The agent takes the mechanical work; people assessment and final decisions stay with the HR team.

The HR market has already moved to AI

In an hh.ru survey of 1,314 companies (late 2025), 57% of Russian employers use AI for HR tasks. Usage breaks down as: hiring 31%, HR administration 22%, learning and development 17%, onboarding 15%, assessment 11%. For 2026 companies plan a new wave of rollouts: +22% in onboarding, +21% in learning, +18% in hiring. Effects are measured by Sk Fintech Hub research: 73% of HR specialists report less time to shortlist; 30% report lower cost-per-hire.

The agent across HR processes

Application screening

The agent matches each application to vacancy requirements and returns a ranked shortlist with rationale per candidate. Market benchmarks show AI can save up to 70% of first-pass screening time, and vacancies close 35% faster.

Interview coordination

Candidate messaging, slot alignment, reminders. The recruiter arrives at a ready interview instead of spending a day on calendar logistics.

HR bot for employees

Leave, certificates, company policies, and other typical questions. Per Gartner guidance, a tuned HR bot closes 60–75% of such requests without HR staff.

Onboarding

Adaptation checklists, answers to a newcomer’s typical questions, stage tracking. Per IBM data, automated onboarding raises new-hire retention by 82%.

HR analytics

Attrition-risk forecasts, hiring-funnel analytics, plain-language questions to people data. This scenario runs on our AI analytics engine.

People decisions stay with humans

HR decisions are more sensitive than most others, so the agent is built conservatively. The machine prepares material: shortlists, summaries, drafts. Only a human can reject a candidate or change an employee status. Selection criteria are written down and checked for bias: the agent scores experience and skills, not gender, age, or surname. Every agent decision can be traced to a reason.

Candidate personal data

CVs and HR data are processed in your perimeter or on Russian hosting under 152-FZ (Russia’s personal-data law). Agent access is limited to assigned processes; actions are logged. Candidate consent wording is configured to legal requirements.

Hiring economics

Cost-per-hire is recruiter hours, job-board spend, and the cost of an open role sitting empty. The agent cuts hours on screening and coordination, speeds closing, and reduces idle losses. Per Sk Fintech Hub, 30% of adopters report lower cost-per-hire. We calculate on your hiring funnel at a free audit.

Pilot in 4 steps

The pilot follows the same method as the sales agent and the other roles in the line: a small process, real data, metrics. That is part of our AI implementation approach.

  1. Choose the process. Usually screening on 2–3 open vacancies, or an HR bot for typical questions.
  2. Set criteria. Your requirements and policies become the agent’s rules.
  3. Pilot with oversight. Recruiters check every shortlist; edits refine the criteria.
  4. Metrics and decision. Time to shortlist, time-to-fill, cost-per-hire before and after.

Team does not know how to work with AI?

That is a normal starting point: per Sk Fintech Hub research, 54% of HR specialists are ready to delegate tasks to AI, but fewer than 30% know how to work with it. The gap closes with corporate AI training: we teach the HR team on your vacancies and policies, not on abstract examples.

What it costs

Price depends on processes, systems, and hiring volume, so we calculate it for your funnel. As a guide, count recruiter hours on screening and coordination per month and multiply by the rate. The estimate is fixed after the hiring-funnel audit.
