Corporate AI Training: the Team Learns on Your Processes, Not Someone Else’s Examples

An online course explains what neural networks are. Our workshop ends with a manager sending their first AI-assembled proposal, and an accountant closing inbound-mail triage in minutes. We teach employees to work with AI on your company’s policies and data, because training is part of AI implementation, not a lecture for a certificate.

Discuss a program for the team Ask a question

Why tools “do not take off” without training

Market figures describe the gap precisely. Per Sk Fintech Hub research in 2025, 54% of HR specialists are ready to delegate tasks to AI, but fewer than 30% actually know how to work with it. An AI skills shortage holds back 49% of companies. Companies buy subscriptions and roll out tools, while employees keep working the old way, and savings stay on paper. Training closes exactly that gap.

How training runs

  1. Role-task audit. We find what an employee’s week consists of and what of that AI can handle.
  2. Tool setup. We prepare prompts, templates, and access for department tasks before the session.
  3. Practice on real tasks. Employees solve yesterday’s tasks with AI, not abstract examples.
  4. Reinforcement and measurement. Homework on the live flow and comparison of time on task before and after.

How we measure effect

Before training we record time on 3–5 typical role tasks. Two weeks after training we measure the same tasks. The difference in hours, multiplied by the rate, is the training effect in money. Per hh.ru, learning and development with AI is already used by 17% of companies, and for 2026 +21% new rollouts are expected: the market is moving from talk to systematic skills work.

What corporate training costs

Market ranges from provider offers in 2025–2026: a one-day workshop for a group of 15–25 people costs roughly 150,000–250,000 RUB; a modular 4–6 week program runs about 300,000–600,000 RUB per cohort. We assemble our price individually by number of roles and groups; after a short brief we prepare a personal offer. Inside implementation projects, training is included in the project estimate.

Training inside implementation

The strongest format looks like this: a process audit finds where AI pays back, a pilot proves effect with metrics, and training hands tools to the team. Then skills land on a working solution, not in notes. For example, after launching a chatbot we teach the team to expand the knowledge base, and bot accuracy grows without our involvement.
