How an AI bot differs from a button bot

An AI bot understands free-text questions and answers from the company knowledge base: price lists, policies, service descriptions. A button bot walks a rigid “press 1” tree; one step off the path and the client is stuck. An AI bot answers the question in any wording and hands atypical cases to an operator with dialogue history.

How we launch a bot

  1. Knowledge base. We gather price lists, policies, and frequent questions so the bot answers from them, not invents.
  2. Escalation scenarios. We define what the bot resolves itself and what it hands to a human.
  3. Pilot on part of traffic. We review real dialogues.
  4. Training on dialogues. The base grows with live questions, and accuracy rises weekly.

From practice. We start with the knowledge base, not scenarios, because 80% of bot quality is the completeness of service and policy descriptions. Scenarios without a base make a pretty presentation and a useless bot.

A bot that grows into an agent

Demand is shifting from bots to AI agents: programs that not only answer but also take actions in systems. We design bots to be expandable: today the bot answers and writes leads to CRM; tomorrow it processes returns and prepares documents. Knowledge base and integrations are reused; you do not pay twice. The agent then becomes part of end-to-end AI implementation in company processes.
