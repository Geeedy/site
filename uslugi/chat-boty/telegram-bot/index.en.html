Telegram Bot for Business: the Audience Is Already in the Messenger

Per telecom analytics, more than 60 million people in Russia use Telegram, and more than half of internet users talk to brands there. “Useful bots” roundups from search solve an entrepreneur’s personal tasks; a business needs its own bot for its own processes: lead intake, client answers, payments, broadcasts. We build those bots as part of our AI chatbots practice.

Estimate a Telegram bot Ask a question

What a Telegram bot for business is

A Telegram bot for business is a program account in Telegram that answers clients, takes leads and payments, handles booking, and sends broadcasts to subscribers. The bot works around the clock where your audience already spends time, and sends every dialog to CRM.

Telegram Business vs a bot: what differs

Telegram Business is a mode of a regular account with business features: greetings, quick replies, working hours. A bot is a separate program with its own logic: scenarios, knowledge base, payments, integrations. Do not confuse them: Telegram Business is set up in an evening and helps personal sales; a bot automates volume. Since 2025 Telegram Business officially lets you connect bots to inbound messages on a business account, so the tools work together.

What the bot does for business

  1. Takes leads and handles booking. The client places an order in the dialog, without a website or calls.
  2. Answers questions 24/7 from the knowledge base: prices, terms, order status.
  3. Accepts payment inside the bot via connected payment services.
  4. Sends subscriber broadcasts: promos, reminders, status updates, with segmentation.
  5. Automates internal processes: employee requests, reports, team notifications.

AI bot in Telegram

A button bot walks menus; an AI bot answers free text from the company knowledge base: price lists, policies, terms. Our knowledge base is shared: the same “brain” answers in the website bot and by phone. Configure once, it works across channels; updates are instant.

Leads to CRM

A Telegram dialog becomes a deal in CRM with chat history, source, and status. The manager picks up the conversation from the card; the client repeats nothing. That works especially naturally with amoCRM, where messengers live inside the deal card.

What about WhatsApp in Russia

Honest answer: the official WhatsApp Business API is available to Russian companies through intermediary integrators, with pre-moderation of template messages and a subscription from about 5,000–10,000 RUB per month per number plus pay-per-dialog. We connect WhatsApp as an extra channel where the audience lives there, but in Russia it is usually better to start with Telegram: cheaper, more stable, and without limits on proactive messages to bot subscribers.

Development stages

  1. Scenarios. What the bot does, where it escalates to a human, what data it collects.
  2. Knowledge base and integrations. Price lists and policies for AI answers, CRM and payment links.
  3. Pilot. The bot runs on part of the audience; you read the dialogs.
  4. Launch and retraining. Broadcasts, analytics, weekly knowledge-base expansion.

Broadcasts without spam

Telegram broadcasts work only for bot subscribers: people who pressed Start themselves. We do not do bought lists or spam broadcasts, because that means a bot ban and brand damage. The right mechanic looks different: a useful bot gathers subscribers, segments by interest, and sends what the person agreed to receive.

Why us

For us a bot is not a builder toy but part of a system: AI answers from a knowledge base, leads to CRM, a shared “brain” with the website bot and voice channel. When you outgrow the bot, it becomes an AI agent that not only answers but acts in your systems.
