Bitrix24 Implementation: Staged So It Sticks

Bitrix24 is Russia’s most common CRM: per J'son & Partners, about half of companies name it as primary. The platform can do almost everything, from CRM and tasks to documents and a portal, and that is exactly its main trap. We implement Bitrix24 in stages: sales first, the rest as the team adapts.

Most SMB companies have enough cloud: a typical launch takes 1–4 weeks. The box is justified with strict data requirements or non-standard customizations.

What we set up

CRM always comes first: pipelines for your stages, fields and cards, role permissions, auto-tasks and robots, manager reports. That is the core that pays for implementation: requests are logged, deals move, numbers are visible.

Integrations: website, telephony, 1C, messengers

Website forms create leads with source tags. Telephony writes calls into the card. Telegram and WhatsApp gather in the platform contact center. 1C syncs invoices and payments. Each integration closes a point where requests used to get lost.

Why turning everything on at once is a mistake

Per industry reviews, 30–50% of CRM implementations miss their goals, and trying to launch all modules at once remains a typical cause. The team drowns in new interfaces, sabotages the system, and goes back to Excel.

Our method is built differently: CRM and sales go first, two to three weeks go to adaptation with discipline control, then tasks and projects connect, then documents and portal. Each next module turns on when the previous one has become habit.

AI on top of Bitrix24

The platform’s built-in AI covers basic scenarios. On top we add AI agents for your processes: lead qualification, call summarization into cards, stalled-deal control. Per an industry report, AI scoring lifts win rate by up to +76%.

Training and adoption

We train on your company’s live deals, not demo examples. The first weeks we control discipline together with the manager, because CRM dies not from settings but from manager silence when people “forget” to log. Policies defining what, who, and when must enter are part of the project.

How much it costs

Market implementation ranges: basic roughly 30–80k RUB, standard roughly 100–300k RUB, advanced roughly 400–900k RUB. We price individually by number of pipelines and integrations. Bitrix24 licenses are paid to the vendor and issued to you; current plans we show at audit.

When Bitrix24 is overkill

Honestly: if the task is limited to a sales team of 2–20 people with short deals, amoCRM will launch faster and stick more easily. Bitrix24 is strong when you need whole-company processes. We implement both platforms, and vendor-neutral comparison is part of the audit.

Why us

We are an independent integrator and earn on implementation and adoption, not licenses. The staged method works against the main failure cause; training runs on live deals; the first weeks are controlled; and if you need another platform, we will say so honestly.
