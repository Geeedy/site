Voice Bots: A Phone That Never Misses a Call

A missed call at midday is a client already dialing the next number on the list. A voice bot answers instantly around the clock, confirms appointments, dials the base, and writes every conversation result to CRM. The economics are simple: a bot minute costs noticeably less than an operator minute.

Send a request Listen to a demo

How a voice bot differs from an IVR menu

A voice bot is AI that understands live speech and answers from the company knowledge base, instead of forcing the client to listen to “press 1, press 2.” The client speaks normally, for example “move my appointment to Friday,” and the bot acts: finds the booking, offers a time, confirms, and writes the result to CRM.

An IVR menu routes button presses. A bot runs a dialogue and solves the task. That is the whole difference.

What the bot does with calls

Takes inbound 24/7

Answers typical questions, books, files requests. In mature rollouts the voice channel closes over 38% of inquiries without an operator, as a banking-sector example shows.

Confirms appointments

Dials the day before a visit for confirmation, reschedule, or cancel. Fewer no-shows, denser schedule.

Wins back “sleeping” clients

Dials the base with a personal reason. Clients who have not bought in six months hear a live offer instead of reading a newsletter in spam.

Qualifies requests

Makes the first scripted call: what is needed, when, what budget. The manager gets a warm contact with a filled card.

Runs surveys

Collects NPS and post-purchase feedback; results go straight into the report.

How much it costs: minute economics

The market pricing model is a one-time setup for your process plus per-minute talk charges. An honest comparison: calculate the cost of an operator minute including rate, taxes, training, and turnover, and compare it with the bot-minute price from our personal estimate. On typical calls the bot wins by multiples; on complex ones the operator is irreplaceable, and the bot does not touch those.

How it works

The bot connects to your telephony, listens and understands speech, answers from the knowledge base, and takes actions: booking, reschedule, request. Every call result lands in CRM with the recording, topic, and outcome. Atypical cases the bot transfers to an operator, passing context.

Is outbound dialing legal

Yes, when rules are followed: dialing runs on a client base that consented to communications, with company introduction and an opt-out path. We account for advertising-call and personal-data requirements when configuring scenarios. We do not do grey lists or spam dialing, because that risks your brand and number.

One brain for text and voice

The voice bot uses the same knowledge base as the chatbot: price lists, policies, answers. Set once, and it works in both channels; answers stay consistent, updates are instant. If you already have a text bot, voice connects as a module instead of being built from scratch.

Launch in 4 steps

  1. Scenarios. We choose calls for the bot: typical and high-volume.
  2. Knowledge base and integrations. Telephony, CRM, booking calendar.
  3. Pilot. The bot runs on part of calls; you listen to recordings.
  4. Scale. We expand scenarios by measured accuracy.

Why us

Our voice bot is a module of AI implementation, not a boxed “robot girl.” Scenarios are designed for your processes, results are measured in minutes and money, and the bot grows with the company’s AI agents.
