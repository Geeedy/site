What a store includes

  • Catalog with filters, attributes, stock levels.
  • Cart and payment: cards, Faster Payments System (SBP), invoices for legal entities.
  • Delivery: carriers, pickup points, tracking, notifications.
  • Personal account: orders, repeat purchases, bonuses.
  • Admin panel: catalog management without a programmer.
  • Speed and SEO readiness: fast product cards, markup, clean indexing.

Integrations: 1C, warehouse, delivery

An order should flow without manual retyping: payment is recorded, 1C reserves the item and prepares documents, the delivery service gets the request, the client gets tracking, and CRM keeps history for repeat sales. Stock sync protects against selling what is not in the warehouse.

There are marketplaces: why your own store

Not “or,” but “and.” Marketplaces bring traffic but take commission, own the client base, and change rules without you. Your own store gives margin without commission, a buyer base for repeat sales, and independence from someone else’s rules. The working setup: marketplaces for reach, your store for profit and loyal customers.

AI modules: search and recommendations

Smart search understands “something warm for autumn” and typos; recommendations show what is relevant. Per an industry report, AI personalization gives +15–30% to conversion. A consultant bot answers product questions around the clock. More on the website with AI page.

Process

  1. Brief and project class. We map assortment, integrations, and load; honestly recommend a class.
  2. Prototype. Catalog, product card, cart: logic before design.
  3. Design and development. Iterations with demos.
  4. Integrations. 1C, payment, delivery, CRM, and end-to-end order test.
  5. Launch. Content load, analytics, access handoff.

What about SEO for a store

Product cards and categories give a store its main source of free traffic. We build category structure for demand, product markup, speed, and correct indexing so promotion starts without rework. For stores SEO pays back especially fast, because demand is hot and transactional.

Why us

A store is a step on the development ladder: open ranges, honest project class, integrations without manual retyping, code and access yours. When you grow to highload, web applications are already our territory.
